Trainer fined for 'using inappropriate language and speaking in an aggressive manner' after altercat

TRAINER Gay Kelleway has been fined after an 'aggressive' altercation with her OWN jockey.

The incident took place at Southwell on Saturday where her horse Mukha Magic was ridden by apprentice Oisin McSweeney.

The five-year-old horse could only manage third in the race, but his trainer Kelleway clearly believed McSweeney could have done better.

The pair were called in by the stewards after the race after two fellow riders witnessed an incident between Kelleway and McSweeney in the unsaddling area.

The post-race stewards report read: "An enquiry was held following a report from two riders who rode in the third race and observed an incident
between Gay Kelleway, the trainer and Oisin McSweeney, the rider of Mukha Magic, in the unsaddling area post-race.

"Having heard their evidence, Kelleway was found guilty of Improper Conduct and fined £250, in that she had an altercation with McSweeney, using both inappropriate language and speaking in an aggressive manner towards her rider after the race."
