Faruk Shaikh Obituary: How Did He Die? Actor Death Cause Explore

Faruk Shaikh died on December 28, 2013, in Dubai, of a heart attack, shocking the film business and leaving a vacuum in the hearts of his colleagues and admirers. Faruk Shaikh was an award-winning Indian actor, television host, and philanthropist. His prominent career in Hindi cinema lasted from 1973 to 1993, and on television from 1988 to 2002. Faruk returned to the film business in 2008 after a short absence and continued to perform till his sad death on December 28, 2013.

Shaikh contributed significantly to Parallel Cinema, also known as New Indian Cinema, partnering with prominent filmmakers such as Satyajit Ray, Sai Paranjpye, Muzaffar Ali, Hrishikesh Mukherjee, Ayan Mukherjee, and Ketan Mehta. Shaikh was honored with the coveted National Film Award for Best Supporting Actor in 2010 for his outstanding performance in Lahore.

Faruk’s Cause of Death: How Did The Actor Pass Away?

Faruk died as a result of a heart attack. Faruk Shaikh died in the early hours of December 28, 2013, while on holiday with his family in Dubai. On December 30, 2013, a burial ceremony was performed in the Millat Nagar Andheri Mosque in Mumbai, attended by several renowned people, including Javed Akhtar and Shabana Azmi. Shaikh was put to rest at the Muslim Qabristan in Four Bungalows, Andheri West, next to his mother’s tomb.

Shaikh was 65 years old and was in Dubai for a performance when he died. His most recent Bollywood debut was in “Club 60.” Sarika, his co-star in the film, expressed her amazement at his abrupt death, despite the fact that he looked to be in excellent health at their previous encounter. They’d even agreed to meet again in the future.

Faruk’s Obituary Details

Faruk Shaikh, who was born on March 25, 1948, made an indelible mark on theater, cinema, and television. In addition, Shaikh rose to prominence as the presenter of the renowned Zee TV program “Jeena Issi Ka Naam Hai,” in which he conducted interviews with a slew of Bollywood celebs.
