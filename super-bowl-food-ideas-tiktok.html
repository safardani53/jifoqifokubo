Score With These Delicious Super Bowl Food Ideas From TikTok

If you're planning to watch the 2023 Super Bowl, check out these game-day food ideas from TikTok that will totally score among your guests.

Whether or not you're a sports fan, Super Bowl Sunday is for everyone! While some NFL fans may be on the edge of their recliner couches watching the Kansas City Chiefs and the Philadelphia Eagles face off in the 2023 Super Bowl, others will be knuckle-deep in the chip bowl.

Don't worry, we're not judging!

Article continues below advertisement

Aside from the game, the halftime performance, and the multimillion-dollar commercials, Super Bowl Sunday is also about the food. Since it's practically a holiday, ordering from your local pizza and wing stop may be a no-go.

Therefore, many people opt to make their own food. If that's what you're planning, these Super Bowl food ideas from TikTok are the perfect recipes for Sunday's game.

Check out these delicious TikTok food ideas for any Super Bowl party!

So, whether you're throwing a Super Bowl party at your house, heading to a friend's, or just watching with your family, we've rounded up our favorite Super Bowl-inspired food ideas from TikTok that will surely be a touchdown for any gathering.

1. Beef and Cheddar Sliders

If you're looking for a simple food staple that both adults and kids will enjoy, check out these baked beef-and-cheddar sliders. To make them, simply put the bottoms of King Hawaiian rolls in a pyrex dish. Then, layer sliced roast beef and cheese on the bread. Next, place the tops of the rolls and pour a mixture of melted butter, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and Worcestershire sauce. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes and enjoy.

2. Pizza Dip

Who doesn't love pizza?

This pizza dip will totally be a fan favorite at any party! Mix cream cheese, mozzarella cheese, parmesan cheese, garlic powder, and crushed red pepper. Add the mixture to the bottom of a baking dish and cover with a layer pizza sauce. Sprinkle on mozzarella and cheddar cheese, top with pepperoni, and bake in the oven for 25 to 30 minutes.

3. Oreo "No Bake" Cheesecake Football Dessert

Game day isn't just about eating dips and wings; dessert is important too! If you're searching for an easy but delicious dessert recipe, look no further. These Oreo cheesecake footballs will impress your friends. Plus, you don't even have to bake them!

To make, simply blend up (or finely crush) a pack of Oreos and mix it with a block of cream cheese. Then, form them into little football shapes and place them on a baking sheet. Put them in the freezer to firm up and melt some chocolate while you wait. Next, dip each piece into the melted chocolate and place them back in the freezer to firm up again. Finally, add white icing to create the football laces.

4. Buffalo Wing Dip

At this point, eating buffalo wings on Super Bowl Sunday has become a staple in many households. But if you're looking to forgo the bone or create less of a mess, try this buffalo wing dip instead. Place cream cheese in a microwave-safe bowl and stir until smooth. Then, add some Frank's Red Hot, mozzarella cheese, ranch dressing, Greek yogurt, and mix. Add in some shredded or canned chicken and microwave for three minutes. Stir and microwave again for two minutes.

Enjoy this dip with crackers, bread, or veggies.

5. Mini Pigs in a Blanket

These mini pigs in a blanket will leave your guests squealing for more.

Grab a packet of hot dogs and cut them into thirds. Then, open a can of crescent rolls and cut each piece of crescent dough into thirds. Roll the dough around each piece of hot dog and place on a baking sheet. For an added bonus, brush each piece with avocado or olive oil, and sprinkle them with "Everything but the Bagel" seasoning. Bake for 15 minutes.

While your dogs are baking, craft your own mustard sauce by mixing together mayo, Dijon mustard, spicy mustard, and honey. Yum.

6. Spinach Artichoke Dip Wonton Cups

Check out these delicious spinach artichoke wonton cups that are the perfect appetizer for any game-day gathering. Combine cream cheese, mozzarella, parmesan, diced artichokes, frozen spinach, garlic, cayenne pepper, salt, pepper, and mayo into a bowl. Coat a mini muffin pan with cooking oil spray and place a wonton wrapper into each of the cups. Next, add one to two spoonfuls of the filling to each wrapper and top with shredded mozzarella cheese. Bake for 10 to 15 minutes, until golden brown.

7. Mini Pretzel Bites

Looking for a recipe that's both easy and delicious? Try crafting these warm, soft pretzel bites... which you'll totally be making from scratch. Just kidding.

Start by rolling store-bought biscuit dough into logs before cutting up bite-size pieces. Next, take your dough fragments and boil them in a mixture of water and baking soda until they float to the surface (about 15 to 20 seconds). Grease a baking sheet, lay out your pieces, and coat them with an egg wash. Finally, sprinkle on some coarse salt, bake for 20 minutes, and voila!

8. Cream Cheese Ranch Dip

You can neither have too much dip, nor too many chips. We hope you're hungry!

For this creamy, chunky ranch dip, you'll want to start with a softened block of cream cheese in a mixing bowl. Add one packet of ranch seasoning, sour cream, dried chives, and mix. Next, pour in some cheddar cheese and crumbled bacon. Bake for 15 minutes and garnish with fresh parsley.

If you catch us scooping this god-tier dip with our bare fingers, mind your business!

Article continues below advertisement

9. Beer Cheese

This garlic-infused beer cheese will pair perfectly with your "homemade" pretzel bites!

Add cheddar cheese, cubed, softened cream cheese, minced garlic, chives, beer, cayenne pepper, and salt to a cast iron skillet. Cook over medium heat. Once the mixture has fully melted, let it thicken over low heat. Finally, top with more chives and cayenne pepper.

We don't worry about cholesterol on Super Bowl Sunday! Enjoy!
