VPRs Kristen Doute looks sad in first public outing since being fired from show along with co sta

FORMER Vanderpump Rules cast member Kristen Doute was pictured in public for the first time since she was fired from the show along with ex best friend Stassi Schroeder.

On Tuesday, Bravo announced that the reality stars will not return next season after it was discovered they once falsely called the cops on VPR alum Faith Showers.

Kristen, 37, wore a black Dodgers baseball cap, grey tank top and little denim shorts as she stepped out from her Los Angeles home.

The shamed TV personality had her two little dogs on a leash as she walked from the driveway to a white Jaguar.

Her facial expression appeared to be somber as she looked visibly upset while getting into the car with boyfriend, Alex Menache.

The longtime friends first began dating in March and made the relationship Instagram official last month.

Last week, Stassi, 31, and Kristen were accused by former cast member Faith, 31, of reporting her to the police for a crime which she was not connected to.

The duo - who had been best friends for years - had a falling out on the latest season over a fight about Kristen getting back together with her ex, Brian Carter.

Faith revealed the pair called the authorities after seeing a report about an African American woman who was allegedly drugging and robbing men in LA.

She dropped the bombshell: "There was this article where there was an African-American lady.

“It was a weird photo, so she looked very light-skinned and had these different, weird tattoos. I guess this woman was robbing people.

"And they called the cops and said it was me. This is like, a true story. I heard this from, actually, Stassi during an interview.”

Stassi was the first to issue a formal apology and Kristen followed her suit soon after.

She wrote on Instagram: “I have been taking some time to really process what I've been seeing, feeling and learning.

“And I need to address something specifically that happened a few years ago with my former castmate, Faith Stowers.

“Although, my actions were not racially driven, I am now completely aware of how my privilege blinded me from the reality of law enforcement's treatment of the black community, and how dangerous my actions would have been to her.

"It never was my intention to add to the injustice and imbalance. I'm ashamed, embarrassed, and incredibly sorry. I will do better. I have to do better," the reality star claimed.

Earlier today, Bravo issued a statement that also announced newcomers, Max Boyens and Brett Caprioni, will not return after past racist tweets were uncovered.

The network said: “Bravo and Evolution Media confirmed today that Stassi Schroeder, Kristen Doute, Max Boyens and Brett Caprioni will not be returning to Vanderpump Rules.”
