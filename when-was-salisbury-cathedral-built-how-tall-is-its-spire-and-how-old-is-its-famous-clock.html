When was Salisbury Cathedral built, how tall is its spire and how old is its famous clock?

THE suspects in the Salisbury nerve agent attack on Sergei and Yulia Skripal have claimed they were in the city sightseeing, including to look at its famous cathedral.

Alexander Petrov and Ruslan Boshirov told Russian state-funded news channel RT they travelled to the "wonderful" city in Wiltshire after recommendations from friends.

When was Salisbury Cathedral built?

The first Salisbury Cathedral was completed in 1092 at Old Sarum, according to the cathedral’s website.

A new site was chosen in New Sarum 1220 and the foundation stones laid on April 28 that year with the main body of cathedral completed in 1258.

Building work was helped by donations from King Henry III of trees from Ireland and his estates in Wiltshire and 12 years supply of marble from a woman called Alice Brewer from her local quarry.

The tower and spire were built between 1300 and 1320.

How tall is its spire?

At 404 feet, the spire has been the tallest in England since the 16th century and dominates the local landscape.

When maintenance was done to the spire workers climbed 332 steps through the cathedral's roof spaces.

They then went a further 144ft up ladders inside the spire before climbing out of a weather door and scaling the final 49ft outside using metal hoops in the spire's masonry.

How old is its famous clock?

The clock dates back to 138 and is the oldest working mechanical timepiece in existence.

It is one of a group of 14th to 16th century clocks to be found in the West of England.

The clock does not have a dial like modern devices but instead strikes a bell at precise times.

It was re-discovered in 1928, restored in 1956 and is currently on display inside the cathedral.
