Jeopardy! fans spot glaring typo during final round as 6-day champ Megan LOSES controversial winning

JEOPARDY! champ Megan Wachpress has lost after six wins on Wednesday.

But fans also spotted a glaring typo during the all-important final round with host Mayim Bialik.

Mayim, 46, is hosting Jeopardy! for her sixth week after Ken Jennings' recent stint and his announcement he'd be out for "months."

The permanent hosting slot is said to be decided by the end of Season 38 in July 2022.

Tonight, attorney Megan was overruled, losing her seventh game after six wins and $60K total.

Jeff Weinstock - a marketing lecturer from Miami, Florida - may have won but fans were more fixated on a glaring typo on the screen.

THIS SPELLS TROUBLE

The FJ clue read: "This author first thought of a parrot before choosing another bird equallly capable of speech."

The spelling slip-up with three "L's" was quickly spotted by quiz show fans.

"Someone tell the #Jeopardy clue writers not to lean on the L button next time," one wrote.

"I guess @Jeopardy forgot to spell check their Final Jeopardy clue… #oops" wrote another.

Most read in Entertainment

"Gah! @Jeopardy misspelled a lil' something!" wrote a shocked third.

"Spellcheck might’ve been a good idea…" fumed a fourth as dozens noticed the error.

The correct response, which both a defeated Megan and new champ Jeff got was Edgar Allen Poe.

But it was an earlier spelling situation that made tonight's gaffe quite poorly timed.

BAD 'TYPE' OF WIN

On Friday, in a now-viral moment, fans claimed contestant Sadie Goldberger was disqualified from a win, which ultimately went to Megan.

The Final Jeopardy! clue read: "Congratulating her on the 1869 release of her biography, Frederick Douglass wrote, 'I have wrought in the day- you in the night."

She responded correctly by writing "Harriet Tubman."

But player Sadie also appeared to have the same response in mind, and she was shockingly ruled incorrect for her script penmanship.

Mayim said: "It looks like you were going for 'Harriet Tubman' but you did not finish, so unfortunately that is not going to be acceptable."

Sadie sadly nodded in agreement but ended up wagering enough to have beaten the current champ.

Hundreds of fans were furious with the results and rushed to social media - as did Megan herself in a rare moment - about the "bad call."

Megan tweeted over the weekend: “It’s probably inappropriate for me to say anything about the judges' call in FJ yesterday (you can see my immediate reaction on the tape).

"But will say I'd love to see Sadie compete again (if she wants to!)."

Fans also found proof on Reddit of a contestant given points for “even worse” writing - not finishing the last name Denver in “John Denver."

A Twitter thread today scored hundreds of replies - one from a former champ - on if writing out FJ responses instead of typing them should stay in place after the unfortunate controversy.

This evening, another fan wrote: "Still haven't forgiven @Jeopardy for their egregious favoritism. #JusticeforSadie."

On top of the hurtful signature snub from Mayim, former champ Megan raised eyebrows for her razor-thin-margin gameplay - and a peculiar pattern.

She has the lowest six-day total of any streak holder ever and she won three of her six games by exactly $2.

Tonight, gaining momentum earlier on, Jeff picked up six correct responses before the first break.

"Oooh Jeff came to unseat Megan. He’s going straight to the bottom of the board. Love that for him," one tweeted on the new player's performance.

By Final Jeopardy, Megan was in last place with $5,600 to middle podium Sarah’s $7000 and Jeff’s $7000.

She then tried her proven strategic betting pattern, wagering $1,401 - which would have made her a $1 winner.

But while she got FJ correct, with $7001 to Jeff's $7000, he hadn't yet revealed his response or wager.

Jeff also got the clue correct and laid down the law with a big bet and $11.2K total.

"I've never been so happy to see someone I like lose at jeopardy," ripped one viewer.

"I'm going to lose my mind if the current Jeopardy champ wins another game by $2" wrote another.

A third Twitter user accused on her streak, "the judges keep assisting her."

And on YouTube, one viewer wrote: "How many times is she going to win by two dollars? The fix is so obvious."

One of Megan's three $2 victories was her first, when she defeated then-champ Eric Ahasic.

Since then, she was clearly trivia-savvy but also "lucky" as many have posted - frequently also winning by strange defaults like players over-wagering in the final moments.

Canadian tattoo-toting tutor Mattea Roach, beloved rideshare driver Ryan Long, Amy Schneider, Matt Amodio, Eric, and now Megan - all from this streak-heavy season - will compete in the Tournament of Champions in November.

MAYIM MAYHEM

Mayim has faced a scoreboard of backlash as host.

Ken and Mayim began filling in for the legendary Alex Trebek after his tragic 2020 passing and a fleet of Season 37 celebrity guest hosts, including now-disgraced Mike Richards.

The fact that the hosting slot is still undecided has earned quite the debate, as the late Alex had hosted since 1984.

The show's ratings nearly doubled to 9.7M viewers when Ken made his first appearance.

When Mayim took over her hosting duties the headcount dropped to 5.9 million viewers during a college championship and indeed, she hasn't bested Ken's ratings since during Season 38.

Mayim was a front-seat passenger for Ryan's 16-game streak - as many players said he was their "favorite contestant ever," it was an empathetically good look for the actress too.

Many fans have found Mayim to be a perfectly palatable presence, but hundreds have tweeted about her off-putting poise during the typically enjoyable Q&A segments.

Mayim, who studied neuroscience, replied to one contestant's earnest autism testing Q&A with a joke: "Glad to hear spitting in a tube went better than taking blood."

Mayim had another joke that flatlined with fans - she told a doctor he was "just an anesthesiologist, not a surgeon."

She also recently made an off-color joke about marijuana and seemed to misunderstand the legacy of the band Queen.

Fans have also found Mayim to be slow with simply ruling responses correct or not in general.

One Twitter user wrote on Mayim's hosting hesitancy, "She's laggy, I think often it's because she's waiting for cues from the producers, but someone said it might be due to her being edited a lot.

"The thing about Ken ... he actually knows the answers...because he's that guy."
