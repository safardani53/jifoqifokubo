Fears for runner given CPR after 'collapsing metres from the finishing line' of 10k race in Weybridg

A RUNNER has reportedly collapsed just yards from the finish line of a 10k race this morning.

Witnesses said that they saw paramedics performing CPR on the man near the finish line of the inaugural Asics Weybridge Sports 10k run.

Get Surrey reported that emergency services treated the man near the finish line, which is just outside of a health club in Walton Lane.

One woman wrote on social media: "He collapsed right in front of me, just meters from the finish.

"One of the spectators caught him and signalled to the medics. Very sad. I hope he pulls through."

MOST READ IN NEWS

Another runner wrote: "Can't stop thinking about the man who had collapsed at #weybridge10k Puts things in perspective. Thoughts go out to him and his family"

The organisers of the event have said they would not be making a statement.

South East Coast Ambulance has also been contacted.
