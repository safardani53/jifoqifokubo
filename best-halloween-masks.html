9 Best Halloween Masks 2021 | The Sun UK

THE nights are drawing in and Halloween is nearly here – are you ready for it?

Get ahead on the scariest night of the year with our round-up of the best Halloween masks of the year (read: the most horrible).

For those who enjoy dressing up and cosplay, there's no better costume opportunity than Halloween.

That's because as well as the option of transforming yourself into a traditional witch, zombie or ghost, you can also find inspiration in a character from your favourite hit horror film or TV show.

Whatever beast or apparition you decide upon, a mask will help you to quickly nail the look without having to buy loads of tricky-to-apply make-up – we've rounded up some of the best Halloween masks we've seen this year so far.

This article and featured products have been independently chosen by Sun journalists. It contains links which are ads, and if you click a link and buy a product we will earn revenue.

Smiffys Old Witch Latex Mask

  • Old witch latex mask with hair and headscarf, £9.99 from Amazon - buy here

While finding a scary Halloween mask for women can be problematic, Smiffy's have outdone themselves with this hideous latex creation which comes complete with fake hair, a headscarf and a massive wart on its long pointed nose.

In fact, this mask is so good that all you'll need to do to complete the look is don layers of knackered old clothes and carry a walking stick.

HAORONG Goonies Sloth Latex Mask

  • Goonies Sloth deluxe latex mask, £8.99 from Amazon – buy here

While some film buffs will take inspiration from new releases, others will turn to classic productions when they dress up this October.

Take this, from the 1980s classic The Goonies – Sloth, the disfigured giant.

With the missing teeth, lopsided eyes, and small tuft of ponytail, this recreation of the famous villain is quite terrible to behold, so don't sneak up on anyone unless you really want to scare them.

Amosfun Wolf Mask

  • Carnival Wolf Head Halloween Mask, £8.99 at Amazon – buy here

Want to stand out from the crowd? Then this engraved half head wolf mask is the one for you.

The metallic finish and decorative design is heightened by the defined, wispy edges that form the outline of a wolf's face.

It’s lightweight and durable, so it won’t weigh you down on the night and the belt can be tightened to fit the mask around your head with ease and comfort.

As well as the faded silver colourway, there's a brushed, blackened gold option.

It's also good value for money, as this mask will have plenty more uses for fancy dress.

Ausuky LED Light Mask

  • Ausuky The Purge inspired LED Glow Mask, £8.49 at Amazon - buy here

Light up the night with this LED glow mask inspired by The Purge. There are ten different colour LED light masks to choose from, all of which are battery operated with two AA batteries.

It's lightweight, flexible and portable, and the controller allows you to change the lights between three modes: constant bright light, fast flashing or a more infrequent slow flash.

As the mask is comprised of wire it can be bent around your face to contort your features.

L&S Prints Faceskinz Fry Up Halloween Mask

  • Faceskinz fry up Halloween face mask, £12.99 from British Made Gifts – buy here

Now here's something original – a breathable fabric face mask that covers your entire face and neck so you're absolutely unrecognisable, with a macabre face on it made from the components of a British fry-up including fried-egg eyes, sausage mouth and er, bacon eyebrows.

Breakfast has never looked so terrifying.

Game of Thrones Night King Mask

  • Game of Thrones Night King mask, £45.29 at Amazon – buy here

It's a steep price to pay for a latex mask with a synthetic lining, but Game of Thrones fans who want to stand out from the crowd this Halloween will be investing in this Night King mask.

An exact replica of the leader of the White Walkers featured in the series, the mask is beautifully detailed, right down to its steely blue eyes and it has been personally approved by the show's producers.

  • Latex bird skull mask, £11.04 from Hollywood – buy here

Why settle for a regular human skull mask when a bird skull is so much creepier somehow?

Team this latex mask with a black hoodie to maximise the fright value.

Release your inner clown with this Joker mask reminiscent of Heath Ledger’s Joker in The Dark Knight.
