ALDI has done away with a popular item – and the news is devastating customers.

Sometimes grocery chains need to make tough decisions when it comes to products in their inventory.

This could be due to poor sales or because of supply issues.

And Aldi is no different, as a fan on X recently asked about Earth Grown Chickenless Tenders.

"Please, why can I not find Earth Grown Chickenless Tenders in my local Aldi anymore? They are objectively the best such tendies."

Unfortunately, Aldi confirmed with a broken heart emoji that the item has been discontinued.

"Gutted," the devasted customer said in response to the grocer.

"Tell your people that it'll mean fewer trips for us to you no hard feelings, but sad."

Earth Grown is Aldi's vegan line of products, which of course do not contain meat, poultry or seafood.

Previously the meatless tenders sold in 10-ounce boxes.

OTHER VEGAN OPTIONS

But at least the vegan options don't end there at Aldi.

The grocer still offers a variety of products under its Earth Grown brand.

This includes chickenless teriyaki bites, tofu, meatballs, burgers, and ice cream.

These products can sell anywhere ranging from $1.75 to $4.50, depending on the product size, variety, and location.

But if you prefer meatless tenders, Gardein is one name brand that is widely available in the country.

At Walmart, the brand's vegan tenders sell for $7.92 in a 15-ounce frozen bag.

MORE ALDI DISCONTINUED ITEMS

But there are plenty more where that came from aside from vegan tenders.

Aldi recently got rid of the liveGfree-branded wraps.

The wraps were previously sold in original and spinach varieties in 10.5-ounce packages.

In addition, they were gluten-free and Aldi said there are "no plans for them to return."

Another liveGFree product that is affected includes the entire bagels lineup.

Previously, customers could buy them in three varieties: everything, cinnamon, or plain, but now they have been all axed.

Also, the Season's Choice potato puffs have been replaced by the crispy tots at Aldi.

The old ingredients consisted of potatoes, vegetable oil, corn flour, dextrose, onion, spice extractive, salt, and dihydrogen phosphate, according to a screenshot posted by online database Open Food Facts.

Aldi has discontinued two snacks under the Specialty Selected brand: the Emmentaler and Gouda cheese bites.

Aldi on the label described both chips that sold in 4.4-ounce bags as having sweet and creamy cheese flavors.
