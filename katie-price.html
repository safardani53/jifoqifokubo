Katie Price Age & Early Life

Katie Price was born on May 22, 1978, in Brighton, East Sussex, England, United Kingdom. Katrina Amy Alexandria Alexis Infield is her full name. She is 45 years old as of 2023. Amy Price and Ray Infield are her parents. Her father abandoned the family when she was four years old. Her mother married builder Paul Price in 1988. Daniel, her elder half-brother, and Sophie, her younger half-sister, are her siblings. Her nationality is British, and her ancestors are English, Ashkenazi Jewish, Belgian, Italian, and Spanish. Price was a student at Blatchington Mill School. She excelled in sports as a student, swimming for Sussex in regional events. She also acquired an interest in horses and horseback riding as a kid. Price was abused by a guy in the bushes of a park when she was seven years old. She dropped out of school when she was 16 years old.

Career

Katie started her modeling career as a youngster at a very early age. She posed for a clothing business when she was 13 years old. In 1996, she was offered a job on Page 3, a topless female glamor model pictorial in British tabloid newspaper The Sun. She adopted the name Jordan in 1996 and made frequent appearances as a model on both Page Three and in men’s lifestyle magazines. At the age of 19, she had the first of many breast enlargement procedures. She kept modeling and appeared in the Daily Star, FHM, the British version of Playboy, Nuts, Maxim, Loaded, Vogue, and Esquire on a regular basis. In 2004, she appeared as herself in the television shows Dream Team and Footballers’ Wives.

Price appeared as a guest host on The Friday Night Project’s inaugural season. In December 2005, she released an exercise fitness DVD, The Jordan Workout. She also appeared in the third season of I’m a Celebrity…Get Me Out of Here! She was featured on Celebrity Big Brother’s fifteenth season in 2015. She was also one of the candidates in the selection process for the United Kingdom’s representative in the 2005 Eurovision Song Contest. In November 2006, she released her first album, A Whole New World. Her autobiography “Being Jordan” ranked #4 on the bestseller list of autobiographies in the United Kingdom.
