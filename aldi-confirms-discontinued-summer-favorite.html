ALDI has confirmed its popular Pina Colada drink is gone from store shelves - and it won't be making a comeback anytime soon.

A Twitter user reached out to Aldi to complain that its specialty wines are gone far too soon.

In this case, she was upset to see the store's Beach Colada brand of pre-made Pina Colada gone already.

"@AldiUSA the specialty wines don’t stay on the shelf long enough for me to stock up on the ones I like… help a girl out and bring the beach coladas back one more time cause this combo is A1," she said next to a picture of both the Beach Colada and a box of Jumex Pineapple Nectar.

Aldi confirmed it would not be helping her out anytime soon.

The retailer cited the dog days of summer as the reason.

While Aldi has discontinued a summer favorite, rival Walmart is making news for revamping its used-goods sales platform.

The company announced that it is expanding its Walmart Restored line of refurbished items with the new Walmart Restored Premium service.

Walmart Restored allows third-party sellers to sell a variety of used and refurbished items.

While Walmart is keeping the regular Restored service, it is introducing a new premium tier with more benefits for buyers.

In order for an item to be offered under the Premium Restored brand, it must have no visible cosmetic damage and come with a guaranteed one-year warranty.

Additionally, the seller must offer 30-day free returns.

Walmart will also attempt to make sure all electronics featured in Premium Restored have battery health of at least 90 percent.

All refurbished items must also be restored by the original manufacturer or a third party approved by the manufacturer.

The next Premium Restored service is good for the planet too, according to Michael Mosser, vice president and category lead, marketplace, Walmart US.

"Keeping products in circulation for as long as possible is more sustainable, in line with our efforts to enable a more circular economy in collaboration with customers, suppliers, and sellers," Mosser said.

"When we take action to reduce unnecessary waste and increase recycling, we’re working to protect the environment for future generations," he continued.
