Most valuable dollar coins in circulation - do you have one worth up to $4,550 in your collection?

CERTAIN coins are more valuable than the spare change they can be found in.

Last year marked the 100 years of the silver dollar, commonly known as the Morgan dollar.

Morgan dollars are one of the most popular coin series in US history for coin collectors.

Some of these silver coins have sold for more than half a million dollars.

The original Morgan dollar was minted from 1878 to 1904.

They were then minted again in 1921.

Many of these variations sell for upwards of $100.

Most Morgan dollars were produced, stored and then released decades later, according to Cointracker.

The coin’s low mintage has added extreme value over the years.

Below are some of the rare dollar coins that have recently sold on eBay.

1798 Draped Bust Silver Dollar - $3,600 

This dollar coin was listed just below a dollar and quickly rose to $175.

From there, it soared to $450 and kept jumping in price by the hundreds. 

There were 23 bids before selling for an astonishing $3,600. 

This silver dollar was minted in Philadelphia and designed by the chief engraver of the US Mint Robert Scot.

In 1793, Mr Scot was influenced by American painter Gilbert Stuart’s portrait of a society lady and turned the coin's image into the ample Ms Liberty called the Draped Bust, according to the seller.

Her likeness remained fixed for a number of years with the exception of an added curl to her hair in 1798 - the same year as this coin.

Between the 1795-1798 dates, prices for all four coins are roughly the same with some minor design tweaks.

However, those tiny changes can have massive impacts on price.

According to the Professional Coin Grading Services (PCGS), among the early silver dollars, the Draped Bust obverse combined with the Small Eagle reverse is likely the rarest and most valuable.

This silver dollar was in circulation, but stayed in very good condition.

Some 1798 silver dollars are going for around $1,500 but can get up to $10,000 depending on certification, grade and design.

1879 CC Morgan Silver Dollar - $922

This rare dollar coin was minted in Carson City, Nevada - one of the lesser known mints.

It was designed by the famous George T. Morgan who was the seventh Chief Engraver at the US Mint and produced the Morgan dollar in 1878.

The mint mark is where the coin can find its value.

There are two versions of this particular coin: rusted or "Capped Die" or the non-rusted or "Capped CC".

This particular dollar coin is the non-rusted version, featuring a clear CC mint mark on the reverse. 

These Capped CC dollar coins are the most common as nearly double were produced, according to the Professional Coin Grading Services.

USA Coin Book estimates the starting price of these dollar coins tend to be just over $250, but can be worth anywhere from $4,660 to $33,975 depending on the condition.

This CC Morgan silver dollar was listed far below its worth at one cent.

It then rose from $3 to $32 and quickly kept gaining traction.

After 69 bids, the coin sold for $921.50.

1892 CC Morgan Silver Dollar - $1,025

This CC Morgan dollar coin was also minted in Carson City and designed by Mr Morgan.

For this particular year, USA Coin Book estimated the starting value at $290.

Depending on the condition, this year can be worth anywhere from $1,718 to $10,116.

The value of this coin comes from its low mintage as only 1,352,000 were produced.

This Morgan silver dollar was listed on eBay for one cent and stayed below a dollar for a few bids until someone noticed its worth and bid $36.

It then jumped to $50 and quickly rose to $100.

After 40 bids, the rare CC Morgan silver dollar sold for a cool $1,025.01.

1893-S Morgan Silver Dollar - $4,550

This coin is particularly rare due to its low mintage, solid strike and great condition.

Also engraved by Mr Morgan, this dollar coin was minted in San Francisco where only 100,000 were produced.

USA Coin Book estimates the value of these coins to be worth $4,512. 

However, depending on the condition, they can sell for $134,449 and go up to $662,419.

This coin was listed on eBay for just below a dollar then quickly rose to $30.

It then rapidly jumped to $100 and grew by the hundreds with each bid.

After a wild 55 bids, the Morgan silver dollar was sold for $4,550.

How to spot rare coins

These Morgan silver dollars are not the only coins that could be valuable.

Make sure to check your spare change for any rare pennies, nickels, dimes, and others as many of these coins could sell for thousands.

Typically, the most valuable coins are ones with a low mintage or an error, as collectors find those coins are some of the rarest.

A great website to check if your coins are worth anything is eBay.

You just need to search the full coin name, select the "sold" listing and then toggle the search to "highest value.”

If your coins are rare and valuable, you can sell them on eBay or go through a specialist site such as Coinappraiser or USA Coin Book.

If you're keen to buy, be aware of potential fakes online.
