Naturist club opens its doors to reveal what goes on in bare-fleshed den where naked people go swimm

A NATURIST club has opened the doors to its bare-fleshed den where naked people go swimming together and play tennis in the nude.

Ashdene Naturist Club in Elland, West Yorkshire, boasts around 200 members and will hold its first ever open day later this month.

Members of the completely legal group are regularly seen enjoying a drink by the riverside wearing nothing but a smile.

The club, which is quietly hidden away on seven acres overlooking the River Calder, has existed for more than 30 years.

Director Clive Edwards said: “People become interested either because as a family they have been brought up in a naturist lifestyle or they have already felt the benefits.

"For example, a lady taking her first skinny dip in the sea, coming out and feeling the warm sunshine on her body instead of a clammy costume.

“Occasionally we may have a married couple coming along, of whom one of the couple may be reluctant to strip off. Generally after a few visits the person will become comfortable and fully join in.”

On August 26, it will hold its first ever open day to mark The Great British Skinny Dip.

When asked what it’s like to be a naturist, Clive said: “Exactly the same as not being a naturist, except weather and circumstances permitting we are naked.

“Naturism is a great leveller. Without clothes, we all look the same, whatever our colour, shape or size.

“It removes the final barrier between your body and outside world and people feel liberated by it.”

Outside Ashdene’s clubhouse, facilities include an undercover swimming pool and a sauna where naturism is complusory.

But unlike many naturist clubs, it’s open all year round.

Visitors who decide they would like to become members will be required to make five further weekend visits before they can be invited to join as a probationer and later a full member.
