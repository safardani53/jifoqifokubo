Ex-England cricket batsman Paul Smith slams cops after teen thug pointed a gun at his daughter in Bi

AN England cricket star has slammed police after he says no alert was issued following a gang which pointed a gun at his daughter's head.

Paul Smith, who played for England in the Hong Kong and Singapore Sixes tournament, said his daughter was targeted as she walked with her dog through a Birmingham park.

He was shocked police did not put out a general alert to warn people of the gang, after his daughter and her boyfriend managed to flee.

The incident where happened in October but Paul spoke out about it last night.

Paul, who played for Warwickshire from 1982 to 1996, said detectives should have given the attack widespread publicity at the time to warn people in Birmingham.

"As someone who has worked with an awful lot of kids and worked with a lot of police officers as well, I was just surprised that there was no sort of alert put out" he said.

His daughter was walking through Birmingham's Sutton Park when the gang approached near Clifton Road.

A gun was reportedly pointed at his daughter's head while another person produced a knife.

The terrified couple ran towards the Wyndley Gym and the gang rode off on mountain bikes into the park.

Paul said he was also shocked at a lack of CCTV in the park and the streets around it.

He said: "In a supposedly affluent area, in front of a youth centre, no CCTV...that's the thing that really gets me.

"In an era of cuts, less policing, no CCTV.

"My main concern is my daughter and her boyfriend and I obviously have to keep an eye on her.

"We just have to see where we go from here really. It's a pretty shocking incident."

West Midlands Police confirmed today they received a call from a man on October 26, reporting he and his girlfriend had been threatened by four men on mountain bikes.

A police spokesman said: "One of them pointed what is believed to be a ball-bearing gun.

"The men made no demands and nothing was stolen. It's understood they rode off through Sutton Park.

"House to house enquiries were carried out and local CCTV viewed but it does not capture the suspects on camera.

"The matter has been filed pending any more information coming to light.

"The men are described as black and in their late teens.

"One was skinny and with a black afro style hair, another was fat, about 17 stone, and 6ft tall.

"Anyone with information should call 101 or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111."
