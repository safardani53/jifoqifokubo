Duane Allman (Guitarist) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Howard Duane Allman is an American rock guitarist, version musician, and founder and chief of the Allman Brothers Band. Born in Nashville, Tennessee, Allman started playing guitar at the age of 14. The Allman Brothers band was formed in Jacksonville, Florida in 1969 and achieved its biggest success in the early 1970s. Allman is best remembered for his short but influential tenure in the band and in particular his ability to play expressive slide guitar and his talents for creative improvisation. A sought-after musician before and during his tenure with the band, Duane Allman has performed with stars including King Curtis, Aretha Franklin, Herbie Mann, Wilson Pickett and Boz Scaggs. He was also a major contributor to Derek and the Dominos' 1970 album Layla and Other Assorted Love Songs. He died in a motorcycle accident in 1971, at the age of 24. In 2003, he was ranked 2nd on Rolling Stone Magazine's 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time.
