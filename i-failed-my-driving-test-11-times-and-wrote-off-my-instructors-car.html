I failed my driving test 11 times and wrote off my instructor's car during one of them

A LEARNER driver who failed his driving test 11 times wrote of his instructor's car during a lesson.

But he's not the only Brit to struggle getting their licence, with some drivers taking 20 attempts to pass.

Jason Walker, of Crawley, was going for his licence when he wrapped his instructor's car around a pole.

Like many inexperienced first-time motorists, he struggled with completing a three-point turn.

Speaking to BBC, Jason said: "It was my first try doing the manoeuvre, and as I was turning towards the kerb the instructor said I was going too fast and shouted at me to brake.

"But I panicked and hit the accelerator by mistake and wrapped the car around a telegraph pole. It was a write-off."

Top tips for passing your driving test

Junctions: Make effective observations before emerging at junctions and into the path of other vehicles. Always make sure it’s safe before proceeding.

Mirrors: Use your rear view mirror and wing mirrors and react to the information. Make sure you use your mirrors early before changing lanes and direction.

Steering: Maintain a steady course and follow the contour of the kerb or roadside.

Turning right at junctions:Position your car correctly. Do not cut the corner when turning right. Watch out for cyclists and motorcyclists, and any pedestrians crossing the road.

Moving off: When you’re moving off from the side of the road, you need to make sure you look around, check your blind spots - and that you’re indicating the right way.

Traffic lights: Make sure you know the rules about traffic signals and follow them.

Positioning: If lanes are marked, make sure you’re in the middle of the lane. Avoid straddling lines.

Responding to road markings:Make sure you watch out for road markings and know the rules about using them.

Reverse parking: Keep control of the car at all times. Practice your reversing to improve your positioning and accuracy.

Despite his accident, the office worker finally managed to earn his driver's licence in 2006 after failing a whopping 11 times.

His story comes after Freedom of Information data released by the DVSA revealed the ten worst learners in the UK between 2009 and 2018.

A British driver set the record for most practical tests taken in a single year in 2016, after finally passing on their 21st attempt.

In 2009, 2015 and 2015, another learner sat 19 tests in each year and failed them all.

UK driving laws means a learner has to wait ten working days before re-sitting their next practical text.

Shockingly, more than 917,000 inexperienced drivers didn't pass their on-road exam last year, after stricter regulations were introduced in late 2017.

Mark Winn, chief driving examiner at the DVSA, said: "DVSA’s priority is to help everyone through a lifetime of safe driving.

"Candidates should only attempt their test when they’ve gained a broad range of experience and are ready to drive safely and independently.

"Anyone who fails their driving test has to wait at least 10 working days to take another. This ensures the candidate has time to undergo additional training and improve any faults noted by the examiner before they take their test again.

"To prepare people better for driving safely on our roads, we changed the driving test in 2017 so that it reflects real-life driving."
