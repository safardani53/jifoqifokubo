Best Vibration Plates 2023 UK

THE best vibration plates don't need to be the most expensive but they can add an extra dimension to your home workouts.

Whether you stand on them, sit on them or even just rest your feet, vibration plates work by sending vibrations through all the muscles in your body.

Studies have found that not only do they help you tone, but they can also increase blood circulation, improve lymphatic drainage and decrease blood pressure.

We've rounded up popular options below that will suit a range of different budgets.

How do vibration plates work?

To use a vibration plate, you simply have to stand on it and choose a setting that's suitable for your workout.

The machine sends high-speed vibrations through your whole body - this in turn is designed to stimulate your muscles at a higher rate than normal, increasing the effectiveness of your workout.

They can help with weight loss, and also work to strengthen and tone individual muscle groups. Depending on how you use the plate, you can also target different parts of your body.

While naysayers argue that vibration plates don't do much of anything, some studies have found vibration plates do fatigue muscles quicker, causing them to work harder. Researchers even discovered they can help combat osteoporosis.

You only need to work out in 10-minute spells when using a vibration plate, so it's an easy piece of kit to fit into a busy schedule.

Do vibration plates work?

If you're looking to tone up and lose weight, then yes vibration plates can help you do just that.

People usually do squats and crunches on these machines to really see the effects, rather than just standing on them, though.

They make your muscles work harder than usual, which results in more toning and muscle strength.

Studies have found that the vibrations can help you lose fat, reduce your blood pressure and make you stronger, although there is little evidence that ordinary exercise without the machine is less effective.

How do vibration plates work?

Vibrations are sent through your body when you stand on the plate, which stimulates your muscles, making them contract really quickly, activating your blood flow.

They can apparently reduce fat around your midriff and activate your liver, boosting your metabolism.

Unfortunately, simply standing on vibration plates is not going to do much, you need to actually work out for best results.

Most people do sit-ups, push-ups, squats and weight-lifting on the machines, but it all depends on what area of the body you want to work on.

Who cannot use vibration plates

If you have problems with your heart, such as heart disease or you have a pacemaker fitted, then you should avoid using vibrating plates as they can aggravate your heart.

Pregnant women, people with pins fitted or those with injuries should also avoid vibrating plates.

If you're unsure about using them, then we recommend speaking to your GP before purchasing.

Those who swear by vibration plates rave about how much quicker they see results when exercising on them, rather than doing it the old fashioned way.
