Shocking moment masked robbers ram-raid a Barclays bank and then flee with wads of cash

MASKED robbers have been filmed ram-raiding a high-street bank before making off with bags of cash.

The trio of crooks used a car to smash into a Barclays bank before speeding off in a second vehicle.

Shocked bystanders watched as the gang carried out the raid just before midday.

One passer-by was even said to have grabbed a fire extinguisher and blasted it at the robbers as they made their getaway.

A video clip taken by a member of the public shows a silver car reversing into the bank before the bystander blasts the extinguisher at the gang.

Onlookers have said one of the masked crooks was armed with a metal bar as the other men carried away bags full of cash on Stratford Road in Sparkhill, Birmingham.

Witness Musuduq Naz, 24, told how he had been out with a pal in the area when the robbers struck.

The shop manager said: "They pulled up in the two cars and then just rammed the shop.

"I was just sat in the car and my mate jumped in and said 'have a look behind you' and it was all kicking off.

"They left the black car and sped off - one of the guys was having to run down the road. I heard they all then ran on foot after a little while.

"The member of the public even used a fire extinguisher on them - Christ knows what he was playing at."

Cops have been searching the vehicle used to ram the Barclays branch after the gang are understood to have later abandoned the car.

A West Midlands Police spokeswoman said they were "called to Barclays Bank, Stratford Road, Sparkhill at around 11.45am following reports of a robbery".

They added: "It is believed that a vehicle smashed into the building and a number of masked men ran from the car into the bank.

"The vehicle has been seized and will be forensically examined. Police are at the scene and continue to make enquiries into the robbery.

"No arrests have been made and enquiries are on-going. No injuries have been reported."
