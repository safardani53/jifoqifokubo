Josh Ho-Sang | Early Life, Parents & Ethnicity

Joshua Ho-Sang was born on January 22, 1996, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, and is the son of Ericka Ho-Sang, a business analyst,  and Wayne Ho-Sang, a pro tennis player.

Although he is Canadian by birth, the hockey star has a slew of ethnicities that he inherits from his parents, such as Chinese, Jamaican, Russian, Jewish, and Swedish.

However, he has the most affinity to his Jewish roots and never forgets to celebrate Chanukah and the High Holidays. Josh grew up in Thornhill, Ontario, a suburban part of Toronto, with his younger brother Khole.

At an early age, the Canadian managed to captivate many hockey enthusiasts in awe, and the pundits even slated him to be better than the previous Jewish players.

Josh Ho-Sang | Career: Teams & Statistics

Being a player of color hasn’t stopped him. Despite all sorts of remarks that flow around when you are a person of color, Josh indeed broke the stereotypic barrier when pundits claimed him to be a promising player.

In 2012, the Ontario Hockey League Priority Selection had a draft, and the Canadian was 5th in the first-round pick. The man was now a Windsor Spitfires player.

Considered a top-rated prospect, Ho-Sang scored 44 points in 63 games for the Spitfires and received 6th rank amongst the other OHL rookies.

In like manner, the young candidate represented Team Ontario in the World U-17 Hockey Challenge in late 2012, recording three goals and two assists. Moreover, Josh scored one goal and provided an assist at the 2014 BMO Top Prospects Game.

Unfortunately, the winger received a 15-game suspension that prevented him from starting the new season with the Spitfires on April 5 of that year.

The penalization came after Josh illicitly fouled London Knights‘ defenceman Zach Bell. However, after a plea, the suspension was reduced to 6 games. 

Further, Niagara IceDogs traded in their player Hayden McCool to the Spitfires to receive the services of Ho-Sang on November 14, 2014. That season, the winger provided 81 points for 60 appearances.

Lesson for lifetime

During an NHL trial, the Canadian had to leave as a punishment for being late for the first day of training camp.

This situation certainly was a lesson for Ho-Sang. He led the IceDogs to an 82-point record out of 66 games to make up for it.

In the same way, the Canadian netted his first NHL goal on March 7, which was a power-play goal against Edmonton Oilers.

Eventually, in the 2017-2018 season, the winger debuted for the New York Islanders. Within that season, Josh had to report for Sound Tigers on October 25.

During his time in the NHL, he recorded four assists out of 6 appearances. Contrarily, the Islanders called him back for an emergency on November 11, 2017.

Although his stay was short, Josh scored 8 points in 15 games. Immediately, the Canadians had to report for Bridgeport Sound Tigers.

Similarly, upon being named the Player of the Week for the Tigers in mid-February, Ho-Sang and his teammate, Michael Dal Colle, reported playing for Islanders again in March 2019.

After being benched, or let’s say being an unused substitute (scratch), the Canadian returned to the Tigers yet again.

In fact, in August 2019, the New York Islanders asked for the winger’s service and provided him with a one-year contract extension.

Thereafter, in September, the NHL-based team put up a waiver on him, and thus would sign with Bridgeport Sound Tigers for the 2020 AHL season.
