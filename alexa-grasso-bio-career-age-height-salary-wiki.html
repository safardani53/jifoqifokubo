Alexa Grasso: Career & Stats

Alexa started her professional MMA career in her homeland, Mexico. The talented striker managed an impressive undefeated record of five wins, of which three came via knockout in the first 18 months of her career.

Consequently, the beautiful Mexican hype was beginning to grow when Invicta FC snapped up Grasso. Subsequently, she made her debut against Ashley Cummins at Invicta FC 8, which she won by unanimous decision.

The 26-year-old stayed with the company for another three fights as Invicta had Alexa contracted for another year.

She won all of those bouts convincingly in a one-sided affair, which only propelled her status in the MMA scene.

As a result, the top MMA promotion UFC swooped in and signed Grasso from under the nose of their competitor, Invicta FC. On her debut for UFC, Alexa faced fierce opposition from Heather Jo Clark.

Just like her previous nine bouts, the Mexican beauty won the fight by unanimous decision.

But, in the 26-year-old’s next fight, she was brought back down to Earth as Grasso endured the first loss of her career against Felice Herrig.

It came as a surprise to many onlookers mainly because the Mexican beauty was the favorite going into the fight.

As the saying goes, “When it rains, it pours,” in her next fight, Alexa missed the weight limit of 115 lbs by 4 lbs.

It was enough for UFC to find the talented striker 20% of her total purse for the event. However, she rose through the problematic situation and managed to win the fight via a split decision.

In her subsequent bout against Tatiana Suarez, Grasso had the worst fight of her career to date as Suarez won the contest via a rear-naked choke in the first round of the bout.

It seemed like the Mexican beauty could not get out of the rot because she withdrew from all of them due to various injuries in her next three fights. What many people don’t know is that MMA fighters live paycheck to paycheck.

Therefore, pulling out of those three fights impacted not only her health but also her earnings.

It was the toughest year of the young MMA fighter’s career as she had never experienced something like this before.

Nevertheless, the Mexican pulled herself together and beat Karolina Kowalkiewicz at UFC 238. It seemed as though Grasso had finally turned a corner, but sadly it didn’t turn out to be true.

Alexa Grasso Robbed

UFC on ESPN+ 17 will forever be remembered as one of the most controversial moments in UFC history because of the fight between Alexa Grasso and Carla Esparza.

During the fight, Grasso had put Esparza on an armbar, a maneuver that can snap one’s arm in half.

It seemed as though Carla had tapped due to extreme pain, but the referee didn’t manage to see the incident. As a result, the fight continued and went the full distance.

However, there was still one more controversy left as when the fight ended, to the surprise of all the fans, Esparza was declared the winner via a majority decision.

Anyone watching the match could have sworn that Alexa won the bout as she dominated every round.

Grasso was rightly angry with the decision as she felt she got robbed. Also, fans all over the world took to social media to voice their displeasure at the result.

On the contrary, Alexa did manage to win the Fight of the Night award. It was the only positive she could take from the scandalous result. However, there was still another twist left in the story.

Why was Alexa Grasso’s fight canceled?

The beautiful striker was set to face Claudia Gadelha, one of the top strawweight fighters in the UFC at the moment. But, it seems as though Grasso is going through possibly the unluckiest chapter in her professional career.

To further highlight, the two fighters, as mentioned earlier, were set to meet at UFC 246 in one of the televised fights at the event.

As it later turned out, UFC 246 was the highest-grossing event of the whole year for UFC. The primary reason is the return of the most eccentric and must-see fighter of this era, Conor McGregor.

However, the fight got canceled because Alexa badly missed the weight limit of 115 pounds by weighing 121.5 pounds. On the contrary, Gadelha came in at a perfect 115.5 pounds.

Consequently, the Nevada State Athletic Commission canceled the fight because fighters must be only 3 lbs apart at the weigh-ins.

Subsequently, the Mexican fighter announced that she will now be fighting in the UFC’s flyweight division. The reason is the difficulties of weight-cutting and the adverse effects on the health of a fighter.
